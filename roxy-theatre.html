Roxy Theatre

Type
Logo & Branding Guide, Pro Bono Client Work
Tools
Adobe Illustrator, Adobe InDesign, Adobe Photoshop
Date
2019-2020

Overview

I reached out to the small business and theatre, The Roxy, requesting permission to create a new logo and branding guide for them. They are a community-run and volunteer-operated theatre in Neepawa, MB that I visited many times during my childhood. Over the course of 2019 and 2020 I researched their history and conceptualized a logo which would speak to their Art Deco history, yet be able to be incorporated seamlessly with minimal expense to their business.

Research

Before doing anything else, I started my research on the theatre and town and made a mind map of what I coukd find and anything that might be relevant. I reached out to a chairperson at the theatre and requested further information about the theatre's business practices and standing within the community.

Sketching

Through 3 rounds of sketching, I explored many concepts and themes. After considering my research and what a user might epect from a movie theatre logo, most of my ideas revolved around typical film symbols such as a film strip or camera, in addition to the iconic structure and details of the building itself.

During the sketching rounds, I consulted others to obtain opinions, implement constructive criticism, and to help narrow down which logo(s) I would end up producing.

Final Concepts

Finally, I had narrowed down the sketches to two options and fully produced them. The first was a combination of a film strip and stalk of wheat, and the other was an inverse capital R which represented the stage curtains and fit within the theatre's existing Art Deco signage.
